Our games

Maasa: Fashion Invasion

Genre: puzzle/logic game

Setting: the game universe is the utopian future world which is on the brink of a disaster. People, who have been spoiled by consumer culture, have refused from all forms of violence and turned out to be completely helpless in the face of the external invasion threat.

Plot: the humanity now lives in the world of the unicorns and rainbows. However, the evil isn’t dormant. The aliens invade the defenseless planet and take a particular interest in the industry of…. art, fashion and beauty! They carry out a plan of turning the Earth into a fashion-farm and use the fashion auras for their evil purposes.

Game parties: the player takes the side of Evil and acts as an alien bot that carries out orders of the main game villain — fish-like alien mutant called Maasa — it is her who commands the bot to steal the auras from earthlings.

Game mechanics: the player sucks out fashion auras from people with a vacuum cleaner-like device. The game incorporates the elements of time-management and tactics. It requires agility, good reaction and logical skills. The gameplay calls for perfecting and practising the skills obtained inside the game as well as learning new rules as the player progresses to new levels and unlocks new content. The success is guaranteed by a correct aura harvesting tactic — the aura needs to be collected in a way that prevents the bot from being spotted and the whole harvest should be gathered quickly.

Maasa: Hysteric Trip to Alpha Release

Genre: arcade/simulator/strategy

Setting: this game is set in the same universe where the alien forces can go at all lengths to farm precious fashion aura from unsuspecting earthlings.

Plot: Once the auras have been sucked, they need to be delivered to various waypoints around the universe, filled with planets where there’s nothing but technical progress and complete boredom. The invaders use the stolen auras to power up their battle fashion robots and carry out trendy gladiator fights in the arenas scattered all across the galaxy. Since logistics don’t happen to be the strong suit of aliens, this leads to a number of goofy and psychedelic adventures as the little spaceship proceeds.

Game parties: the player now is an employee of a Holy Ship! Postal service who tries his best to get the fashion to the destination under a vigilant eye and hysterical command of our old friend — fish-like alien mutant called Maasa.

Game mechanics: the player makes decision based on economic feasibility and thus forms logistic routes between planets and even creates trade routes. The player also encounters a number of dangers he needs to cope with — in case of successful task completion the player will deliver the fashion at hyper speed, bringing it to every destination in time and receiving bonuses for it. The player may also buy additional spaceships and build his own transplanetary trading empire. Discovering new planets will help unlock battle arenas in the third game.
